How Do Hackers Crack Passwords?

There are multiple ways to crack passwords. The most popular of which are the brute-force and Dictionary attacks. Both of which involve a script that automates attempts, but in different ways.

Brute-forcing

A brute-force attack tries every possible permutation of characters (which our strength checker calculates). However, don't let the big numbers fool you. Some password crackers using powerful GPUs can run 100 billion attempts at once. Making cracking passwords incredibly easy if they're not secure. It is calculated by (Number Of Possible Characters) to the power of (Length Of Password). If the length of the password is variable the add each permutation (i.e., 94^8 + 94^7 + etc...). Also, keep in mind that the calculated number is the worst-case scenario. So something like 1234 has many combinations, but is usually the first guess, making it even more vulnerable than you might imagine initially.

Dictionary Attacks

Dictionary attacks are similar to brute-force attacks, as a huge number of attempts are done to get the correct password. However, instead of the slow and tedious attempts of every potential combination of inputs, word lists that contain common passwords and passwords found in data breaches before. Dictionary attacks are a much faster way to crack a common/easy password and do not take nearly as much time. Our calculator compares your input to a select number of word lists. If your password appears even once in a word list, you know it's horrendous and can be cracked in an instant.

Safety Measures You Can Take

Always enable Multi-Factor Authentication, so even if your password gets cracked, the hacker cannot access your account. Regularly update your passwords and use strong ones with various combinations of characters to maximize the amount of time needed to crack your password. Never EVER use simple passwords and always create something complex. Password managers are also good, or just do what I do: keep them in plain text somewhere that's well hidden away from everyone else.
