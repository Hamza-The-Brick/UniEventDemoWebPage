What is Phishing?

            
Phishing is the act of luring users with whatever methods that allow the hacker to either access or steal their personal information. Luring can happen through
                emails, SMS messages, Suspicious links, and many more. One might underestimate how powerful phishing can be, thinking the general public should be aware by now not to trust
                whatever is sent to them online. But people still fall for it every day.
            

            
Social Engineering

            
Creating the malicious emails and links is one thing. Placing the actual bait is another. People often fall for such scams because the emails and messages can carry a sense of urgency and danger, making the user feel.
                want to act as fast as possible. Phishing can also happen through compromised friends' accounts, so you get a message from your friend asking you for money or to run a peice of software when it actually is a scam and malware, respectively.
            

            
Safety Measures

            
Never EVER click on links, run software, or answer calls from unknown numbers. All of those are dangerous and can lead to theft of your personal data and accounts. Train paranoia by default, and always be
                Suspicious of things sent to you online, and build a habit of verifying them through anti-virus tools. Try out our quiz above to see how good you are at online safety practices.
