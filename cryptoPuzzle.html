Brief History

Cryptography goes back thousands of years, from Egyptian hieroglyphics, to the Mesopotamians encrypting clay tablets that have intellectual property and to ancient Greeks tattooing messages on a solider's head so it can only be read after his head is shaved by the recipient. Humans have been using stuff to hide stuff for centuries (known as classical cryptography). Within the modern age and the dawn of the computer, encryption has become more essential and much more complicated. Nowadays, it isn't a simple matter of shifting; entire algorithms are written to protect data and hide it.

How Does It Relate To CyberSecurity?

Cryptography and encryption are essential in the field of CyberSecurity as encrypting sensitive data and recognizing the potential of hiding malicious code within seemingly normal files are a must-know for any CyberSecurity expert. Malware forms such as Trojans and Ransomware rely on principles of Cryptography to hide their malware inside normal-looking files and encrypt the user's data for ransom, respectively.
